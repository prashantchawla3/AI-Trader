One of the ways that I approached financial independence was by recognizing the realization that I'm actually not only okay with earning less, I would love to earn less if it meant that I could live life on my own terms. So, think about how many people were on unemployment during the pandemic and said, "You know what? I'd actually rather continue collecting unemployment and yeah, I might not be making as much as I was when I was at work, but this is actually a lot better." Well, I sort of had that same experience in my own journey to financial independence where I realized, you know, I could live with less and I would be happier. I found that the high monthly burn rate created this burden that really was enslaving me to that 9 to 5 grind. And if I could tell myself, listen, I don't need to drive a brand new car. I don't need to have as many fancy things as the next person.
