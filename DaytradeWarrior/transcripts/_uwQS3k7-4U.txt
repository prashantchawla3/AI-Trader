You have to deal with big emotional ranges. The ups from having a great day, but the downs from having a bad day. This is a job where you can come in and you can work a regular, you know, full work week and walk away with less money than you had at the beginning of the week. It's not like that with really any other job, right? You have your flat hourly rate, you get paid no matter what, for the most part. But with trading, you can get rewarded big time when things are good, and you get a real big boost of confidence, but when things aren't going well and you have that drawdown, that can hurt your confidence and then that can get you frustrated, get you angry, and then it can lead to impulsive trading, which is when you start to spiral. Now, pro traders know all of this, and they've learned to become highly disciplined.
