So, a more bullish candle would be one that doesn't have an upper candle wick at all. It just simply squeezed all the way up to this high and then closed right there. Naturally, that would be a much stronger candle. Even if it still had this lower candle wick because this lower candle wick or this lower shadow, while it shows that the price did decline here, it shows that the buyers bought it back up, which is bullish. So in total this bottom candle wick and lower shadow is bullish and an upper candle wick or an upper shadow is bearish.
