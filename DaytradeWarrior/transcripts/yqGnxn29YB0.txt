Liquidity refers to the presence of other traders in the market. Which means the second that you want to buy shares, there's another trader out there willing to sell to you. That person selling to you is actually adding liquidity to the market. The presence of their sell order is creating a fuller market. Now, on the flip side, when you want to sell shares, the presence of another trader willing to buy, that trader is creating liquidity. So the presence of other traders is what creates a liquid market. In contrast, an illlquid market, and this is very common with penny stocks, would be a security where there's really nobody buying and selling it. There's nobody trading it. So, if you own a position and you decide you want to sell, it could actually be difficult to unload that position if there are no buyers.
